COLOMBIA TEMPLE

The Temple of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints in Bogotá, Colombia, is one of the temples built and operated by the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, number 57 in operations by the church, being the first in operations in Colombia until the dedication of the Barranquilla temple, in the last general conference a new temple was announced in the city of Cali. Announced in 1984 along with four other temples, it would bring the number of temples in the world to a total of 47, setting a record for temple constructions for the time, having built the same number of temples in three years as in previous years since its foundation in 1830. THE CANDELARIA

La Candelaria is the vibrant center of Bogotá, with iconic landmarks like a colonial-era cathedral and the neoclassical Capitol that flanks Plaza Bolívar. Narrow streets lined with shops selling emeralds and handicrafts lead to cultural landmarks such as the Museo del Oro, with pre-Columbian artifacts, and the Museo Botero, which showcases international art in a colonial mansion. SITUATION IN COLOMBIA

The protests in Colombia in 2021 (or National Strike in Colombia 2021) are a series of multifactorial demonstrations carried out, mainly, with the aim of suppressing the tax reform project proposed by the government of Iván Duque, due to the COVID-pandemic. 19. Several of the claims of these protests, mobilizations and demonstrations are the cause of social discontent, this discontent is due to the low budget in health, education, and the mismanagement of the national budget. The demonstrations have been affected by vandalism and violence between citizens and the national police. Protest
