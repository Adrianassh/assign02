Cartagena

Since the time of the colony, Cartagena has transmitted its magic with its culture, its people, in addition to its strategic port for the continent, trade on this side of the hemisphere flowed through this area of the Caribbean, Galleon caravans sailed our seas. Cartagena still continues to feel strategic; its port is still the best in the Caribbean, it is also within the ranking of the 100 best in the whole planet and knowing more about this beautiful city will be worth every minute invested.



HISTORY

Pedro Heredia, part of a noble family in Spain. He was a conqueror. He was born in Madrid around 1520 and died in the Atlantic Ocean in 1554. He explored the coast and part of the interior of Colombia. Due to some political scares and some appointments that he had decided to found the city of Cartagena, although this happened between January 20 and 21, 1533, the official date of the foundation of this great city was June 1 of the same year. Cartagena was key to the independence of Colombia, starting with something called the Revolution de los Commoners, where around the years 1752-1764 and 1767 the first mutinies against the Spanish yoke were presented, initially they only wanted the tax burden represented in taxes and that drowned the inhabitants of the colony. To stop these riots, the Spanish decided to kill each leader of the community members to avoid further revolts. A vase, a simple vase became the symbol of every Colombian as it unleashed the cry for independence from Colombia on July 20, 1810 in the city of Santa Fe de Bogota. After more than three centuries of conquest of the Spanish empire, the independence feeling spread in America. Cartagena achieved its total independence on November 11, 1811 and from then on it was key to the independence of the entire country.



WEATHER

Throughout the year the climate of Cartagena is hot, it is a tropical climate, although there are some minimal changes that go according to seasonal temperature changes, and this is mostly due to the amount of rain that falls more than the temperature of the city. therefore, two periods in the year can be easily distinguished. The first runs from December to April, in which it only rains 6 days, and the second from May to November, with October being the month in which it rains the most and the month in which it is least recommended to travel to take vacations, approximately 90 days. of rain, however the temperature of the city does not vary, having a maximum temperature of 30 C to 31 C, in fact the lowest temperatures that could occur range from 23 C to 25 C. The humidity is 80%, however the blowing of the wind, especially after the sunset, brings a refreshing relief for those who visit the city.
