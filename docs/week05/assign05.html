INDIGENOUS VILLAGES

Ethnic groups present
In the entire Colombian Amazon region there are approximately 26 indigenous ethnic groups with a population of approximately 47,000 members. 14 linguistic families stand out, among which the most numerous are the Tukano, Arawak, Tikuna, Huitoto and Tupí families. The calculations are still very approximate due to the difficulty of knowing exactly the movements of the populations in the area. Such is the case of many indigenous people who live in cities or urbanized centers, who have lost their language and their identity, or groups such as the Urumi or the Yurí whose location is hypothetical.
